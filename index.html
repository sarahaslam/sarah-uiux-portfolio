Academic Project · Human computer interaction

FAST Park

A full UX case study for a campus parking app that helps students, faculty, alumni, and visitors find, reserve, and navigate to parking spaces.

Prototype ↗ Figma file ↗

Problem

Campus parking was stressful, unpredictable, and poorly communicated.

I chose a real issue from my own university instead of a generic app prompt. The challenge was to reduce the time and mental effort users spent finding parking, especially during peak hours, events, and class timings.

Research insights

Students

Needed a faster way to find spaces, especially when late for class or during peak times.

Faculty

Valued real-time parking updates and a straightforward interface that does not demand too much attention.

Visitors

Needed clearer guest parking, signage, reservations, and navigation because they were unfamiliar with campus.

Information architecture

The app was structured around five practical areas: account, reservation, real-time parking, maintenance, and help/support. I explored both hierarchical and network navigation to keep frequent actions close to the home screen.
